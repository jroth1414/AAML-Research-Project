# Dev-Plan Adversarial Review (provenance)

_Auto-generated record of the adversarial review pass used to harden this plan. Critical/high findings are folded into the Risk Register in `../../DEVPLAN.md`._

## Overall assessment

The plan is unusually thorough and largely executable: Phase 0 (setup), Phase A.2 (finance/macro), and Phase D (eval/stats) are strong, with concrete signatures, leakage guards, and acceptance criteria. The capability-gating for Mamba/foundation models is realistic and the CPU-first ordering is sound. However, the plan is NOT cleanly executable as written because of (1) several hard cross-file data-contract inconsistencies that will break the M->E and P->M seams (date/index conventions, prediction-file path and column-name mismatches, results-store schema collisions, FRED-map ownership conflicts between F6 and P1), (2) a genuine statistical-power problem the plan undersells: with ~144 months, a 60-month min train + 12+12 val/test at step 12 yields only ~6 folds, and the dense step-1 walk-forward produces overlapping/serially-correlated test points that the DM test treats as independent -- and ragged ETFs (XLC ~78 months) cannot support 60-month training in early folds at all; (3) an unresolved leading-task alignment ambiguity (whether the natural +1 month is in addition to or the same as the release lag) that is stated inconsistently across N7, F12, and P5; and (4) the iTransformer "variate view" as specified rests on each sector having multiple simultaneously-valid regions, which after the correlation pre-screen and ragged histories may collapse to <2 variates for most sectors, undermining H2/H3 testability. The biggest strengths are leakage-awareness and reproducibility scaffolding; the biggest weaknesses are inter-file contract drift and honest small-sample/multiple-comparison power accounting. These are fixable by the editor with targeted reconciliation, but they must be fixed or the autonomous agent will hit contradictions it cannot resolve.

## Missing topics flagged

- No transaction-cost / Sharpe handling for ragged-ETF and multi-ETF portfolio aggregation: E3 strategy_returns is per-series with prev_pos chaining, but how the 11 single-ETF long/short strategies combine into the headline gross/net Sharpe (equal-weight portfolio? pooled?) is unspecified, and the net-Sharpe sensitivity to the 10bps cost given monthly sign-flipping is not analyzed.
- No explicit handling of FRED data revisions / vintages beyond a note: the nowcast uses final (revised) IP values, which is a real look-ahead relative to a true real-time nowcast. P5 mentions ALFRED as optional and W10 lists it as a caveat, but there is no concrete plan or acceptance criterion, and it materially affects the H5 claim's validity.
- No directional-accuracy significance test: H1 requires dir_acc>0.50 but there is no binomial/sign-test on whether dir_acc is significantly above 50% (only DM on MSE). With small T this matters; a sector could show 0.55 dir_acc that is not significant.
- No spatial/region multicollinearity or feature-redundancy treatment: NTL features (mean/median/sum/p90/lit_count/lit_frac) are highly collinear; nothing addresses which feature(s) feed the model or whether all are used as separate CI series (inflating the pool with redundant series and the multiple-comparison family).
- No concrete compute-time budget validation: M5 says PatchTST ~30 min/config on CPU and HP grids are large (L x lr x batch x dropout x model-knobs x tasks x H x folds). The product could be hundreds of CPU-hours; there is no total-runtime estimate or a hard cap/early-abort to keep the autonomous run tractable.
- No data-availability contingency if VNP46A3 for some region-months is mostly gap-filled (high frac_masked) across many months (e.g. high-latitude winter, persistent cloud): N flags frac_masked but no rule states the minimum coverage for a region-month to be usable or what fraction of dropped months invalidates a region series entirely.
- No validation that the 11 single-ETF leading-return targets are not dominated by a common market factor: predicting sector returns largely reproduces predicting the market; a market-excess (sector-minus-SPY) or factor-neutralized target is never considered, which affects whether any 'signal' is sector-specific NTL information vs beta.

## Cross-cutting risks

- DATA-CONTRACT DRIFT is the dominant risk: month-start vs month-end index, prediction-file path/columns (predictions.parquet vs predictions/*.parquet; etf vs series_id; missing pretrained/seed/split), results-store name/schema (results.parquet S8 vs results_store.parquet E1), VIX columns (vix_max promised by P, never produced by F), and three diverging manifest schemas. The master plan must add a single normative 'Data & Artifact Contracts' appendix and make every phase cite it instead of re-declaring.
- OWNERSHIP CONFLICTS for shared config files: configs/sector_fred_map.yaml (F6 vs P1) and configs/regions.yaml (N4 vs P1) are each authored twice with different schemas and even different bbox coordinates; configs/hypotheses.yaml is cited by W but created by no one. Assign exactly one owning phase per shared file and make others consume+validate.
- STATISTICAL POWER / MULTIPLE COMPARISONS honesty: ~144 months -> ~6 folds at step 12; dense step-1 folds give overlapping, serially-correlated, cross-sectionally-correlated test points that DM (with h-1=0 HAC lag at H=1) treats as independent.
